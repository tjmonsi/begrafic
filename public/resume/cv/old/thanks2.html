Dear Geoff:

Thank you for the opportunity to talk with you today about the Creative Director position. The more I learned about your group, the more convinced I became that working with you would be the ideal arrangement for me.

While I would be interested in the webmaster position, I believe that my experience and areas of expertise and interest make me ideally suited for the creative director position. I would be eager to lead the graphic design team in accomplishing a number of immediate goals, some of which include establishing the Traffic Station brand name with a new logo and identity, as well as designing a new look and cohesive feel to the entire site. I also believe that an intranet and/or printed newsletter for clients, investors, and government groups would establish greater continuity in Traffic Station's public relations efforts.

In addition, I can help in translating new sites into Spanish for Traffic Station's Florida, New York, Chicago and LA markets. My journalism background has given me an excellent eye for editing, both in English and Spanish.

The broad range of creative possibilities makes this job very appealing. I have always enjoyed being a part of collaborative projects, and I feel that I could contribute a lot of energy and enthusiasm toward the work assigned me.

I am confident that, should you decide to hire me as creative director, I will be capable of taking on all of the responsibilities that this position requires, acquiring and improving new skills, and meeting challenges and deadlines in a professional manner. Thanks again for your time. I look forward to hearing from you soon.

 

Sincerely,

Beatrice Aispuro
