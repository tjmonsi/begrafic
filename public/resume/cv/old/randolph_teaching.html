Dear Peggy Bilbro and Jerry Beckman,

I enjoyed my visit to Randolph School last week. Thank you for showing me around and introducing me to your staff. Randolph reminds me of my elementary school, San Gabriel Adventist Academy in California. I was very impressed with the quality of instruction and the well-rounded program exhibited at your school.

I am interested in applying for a Spanish teaching position and substitute teaching at Randolph. I received my Bachelor of Arts degree from CSULB in Journalism and minor in Spanish in 1991. Since attending Art Center College of Design in Pasadena and beginning my web/graphic design career many years ago, I have had the opportunity to work for many design firms in a variety of capacities.

As the first webmaster and designer at Caltech, I collaborated extensively with staff and faculty in creating and developing promotional material, both online and in print. I also taught classes for staff and students in constructing web pages, scanning, photographic manipulation, and design. As a native Spanish speaker, I was responsible for translating documents and facilitating bilingual discussions for Caltech geologists conducting research in Mexico. At Shopsports.com, I translated all store web sites into Spanish. I served as translator for two latin magazines; Latin Athlete and Latin Holiday and have translated for many screenplays.

I taught kindergarten and second grade for several years while attending college and I have had vast experience teaching in an industry setting. Not only have I conducted classes in design and software use, but I have worked one-on-one with students in hands-on learning situations, some of which required my bilingual skills. These experiences have increased my desire to become involved in a formal teaching environment again.

I am extremely organized, detail-oriented, resourceful, proactive, and energetic. I look forward to speaking with you soon to discuss possible teaching opportunities at Randolph School.

Sincerely,
Beatrice Aispuro
