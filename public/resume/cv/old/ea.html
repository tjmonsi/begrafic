Dear Eric Ayzenberg:

I enjoyed speaking with Esteban last Friday, but regret that I didn't get an opportunity to talk to you about the design position you had available and to show you some of my work. I was looking forward to meeting you because I was very impressed with what I learned about your group from the website. I went to New York over the weekend and had a chance to visit the Guggenheim while I was there. (I regret not being able to chat with you about it!) It was amazing, overwhelming. I am more impressed with Frank Lloyd Wright's work than I ever was before. I notice a definite similarity between yours and Wright's philosophy of design----that every element in the design matters----for Wright, to render the most aesthetic and useful living experience; for you and your company, to help your clients make the most effective impact on their customers. I respect that approach very much.

Esteban mentioned that one of the first responsibilities of your new hire would be to help tackle a backlog of work that you have. If there is anything I can do to help, I would consider it a pleasure to do contract/freelance work for you. Please do keep me in mind.

 

Sincerely,

Beatrice Aispuro
