Constructive Critique::
There are spacing issues on different pages. The top graphic sometimes puts the html text beneath the graphic so the entire text is not totally visible. There is a mix of serif and sans-serif fonts. The photographs of people look bland (not properly color-corrected) and they weren't isolated from their background very well; pixelization around the body and hair (even bullets on nav bar appear pixelated).

The colors are a bit "clinical," and often don't match each other and there are not enough inspiring photos of people. People like to see pictures of people they can relate with. The more pictures you show them, the more they can see themselves there.

At all of the dot.coms I worked at, and there were far more than on my resume, we used the color ORANGE a lot. Studies show that the colors of the SUN make people feel good, cheerful, alive,,, Very often I was forced to stay away from BLUES as they emit bland, depressing feelings. These companies spent a lot on research. You see successful businesses like AT&T, CNN Morning Express, etc. use mostly ORANGE/YELLOWS. (just an fyi).. I noticed your site is mostly blue/purple/grey...

I definitely like the shapes used (Funny, b/c I used a similar shape (curves) at the bottom of my casa121 site. Notice also, when I add an external link to my site, it automatically opens up a new window. Your site has external links that will drive traffic away from your site without a way to return. I doubt the user will hit the back button hundreds of times to return to your site once they finish looking through the site they navigated to from your site.

All in all, your site could use some fine-tuning to make it SHOW OFF your work rather than merely explain it. I know I could really make it classier and warmer.
